South Miami Election

Philip K. Stoddard was elected to his third term as mayor of South Miami on Tuesday in a landslide victory over opponents Valerie Newman and Rodney Williams.

Stoddard, a biology professor at Florida international University, received 1169 votes, followed by Newman, a women and South Miami city commissioner for the last five years, with 431 votes and Williams, the owner of a local hair salon, with 275 votes.

"I feel pretty elated," said Stoddard in the commission chambers after the results were announced. "Not only did I win, I got people I can work with. People who share my values."      See More >>

Major Delrish Moss

Walking home from work when he was 15 years old, a police officer pushed him up against a wall, searched him, and walked away without an apology. From that moment, Major Delrish Moss knew he wanted to become a police officer so others would not have to be humiliated in that way.

Moss has been an officer with the Miami Police Department for 30 years. He started his time in the department as a uniform patrol officer driving in his childhood neighborhood, Overtown, a predominantly black neighborhood North of downtown. He worked the midnight patrol for almost three years. According to Moss, this assignment gave him a unique understanding of the problems that plagued Overtown's residents.      See More >>
